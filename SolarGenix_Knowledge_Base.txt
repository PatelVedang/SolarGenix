intelligence and data science to provide:

  • Accurate predictions of how much electricity your solar panels will generate.
  • Forecasts of your upcoming electricity bills based on past consumption.
  • Recommendations on how many solar panels you need to reduce your bill to a
    target amount.
  • An AI-powered chatbot that answers your questions about solar energy, our

Think of SolarGenix as your personal solar energy advisor — available 24/7,
completely free to use, and backed by real data and machine learning models.

SolarGenix does NOT sell solar panels. It is an information and prediction tool
that empowers you with knowledge so you can make informed decisions before
purchasing or installing solar equipment.

WHAT YOU GET BACK:

The system will show you:

  • Current Units — How many kWh/units you are currently consuming.
    (Calculated from your bill using Indian tariff slabs.)

  • Target Units — How many kWh/units your target bill corresponds to.

  • Units to Offset — The difference between current and target units.
    This is how much solar needs to generate.

  • Recommended Solar Capacity (kW) — The solar system size you need.
    Example: "2.5 kW" means you need a 2.5 kilowatt solar system.

  • Recommended Number of Panels — How many 540-watt solar panels you need.
    Example: "5 panels" means you need five 540W panels.

  • Estimated Monthly Generation — How many units the recommended system
    will produce each month.

HOW THE CALCULATION WORKS (IN SIMPLE TERMS):
---------------------------------------------
The system uses Indian electricity tariff slabs to figure out how many units
(kWh) your bill represents:

  ┌─────────────────────┬──────────────────┐
  │ Units Consumed      │ Rate (₹ per unit)│
  ├─────────────────────┼──────────────────┤
  │ 0 – 50 units        │ ₹3.00            │
  │ 51 – 100 units      │ ₹3.50            │
  │ 101 – 200 units     │ ₹5.00            │
  │ 201+ units          │ ₹7.00            │
  └─────────────────────┴──────────────────┘

  Assumption: 1 kW of solar panels produces approximately 120 units per month
              (Indian average).

  Panel size: 540 watts (0.54 kW) per panel — standard size.

Example:
  Current bill: ₹3,500  →  approximately 507 units consumed.
  Target bill:  ₹500    →  approximately 143 units.
  Units to offset: 507 - 143 = 364 units.
  Solar capacity needed: 364 ÷ 120 = 3.03 kW.
  Panels needed: 3.03 ÷ 0.54 = 6 panels (rounded up).

14. INTRODUCTION TO SOLAR ENERGY
==================================

Solar energy is the light and heat that comes from the Sun. It is the most
abundant energy source available on Earth. Every hour, the Sun delivers more
energy to our planet than all of humanity uses in an entire year.

Solar energy is harnessed using technologies like solar panels (also called
photovoltaic panels or PV panels). These panels convert sunlight directly
into electricity that can power your home, charge your devices, run your
appliances, and even feed energy back into the electrical grid.

Solar energy is considered "renewable" because the Sun will continue to
shine for approximately 5 billion more years — making it, for all practical
purposes, an infinite energy source. Unlike coal, oil, or natural gas, solar
energy does not produce any pollution or greenhouse gases when generating
electricity.

India is one of the best places in the world for solar energy. The country
receives 4 to 7 kWh/m²/day of solar radiation — far more than countries
like Germany or the UK, which are already major solar energy producers.


15. HOW SOLAR PANELS WORK
===========================

Solar panels work through a phenomenon called the "photovoltaic effect."
Here's a simple explanation:

Step 1 — Sunlight hits the panel.
  Solar panels are made of special semiconductor material, usually silicon.
  When sunlight (which is made of tiny particles of energy called "photons")
  hits the silicon cells, it transfers energy to the electrons in the silicon.

Step 2 — Electrons start moving.
  The energy from the sunlight knocks electrons loose from their atoms. This
  creates a flow of electrons — which is electricity! More specifically, it
  creates direct current (DC) electricity.

Step 3 — The inverter converts DC to AC.
  Your home appliances use alternating current (AC) electricity. A device
  called a "solar inverter" converts the DC electricity from the panels into
  AC electricity that your appliances can use.

Step 4 — Electricity powers your home.
  The AC electricity flows through your home's wiring and powers your lights,
  fans, refrigerator, TV, washing machine, and all other appliances.

Step 5 — Excess goes to the grid (optional).
  If your panels produce more electricity than you use, the excess can be
  sent back to the electrical grid through a "net meter." You get credits
  on your electricity bill for this exported energy.


16. TYPES OF SOLAR PANELS
===========================

There are three main types of solar panels:

A) Monocrystalline Panels
  — Made from a single, pure crystal of silicon.
  — The most efficient type (20–24% efficiency).
  — Dark black in colour.
  — Last 25–30 years.
  — Cost: ₹30,000–₹45,000 per kW.
  — Best for: People who want maximum output from limited roof space.

B) Polycrystalline Panels
  — Made from multiple silicon crystals melted together.
  — Slightly less efficient (15–18% efficiency).
  — Blue-ish colour with a speckled appearance.
  — Last about 25 years.
  — Cost: ₹25,000–₹35,000 per kW.
  — Best for: Budget-conscious buyers who have enough roof space.

C) Thin-Film Panels
  — Made by depositing a thin layer of photovoltaic material on a surface.
  — Least efficient (10–13%) but lightest and most flexible.
  — Can be made semi-transparent.
  — Last 15–20 years.
  — Cost: ₹20,000–₹30,000 per kW.
  — Best for: Large commercial installations or curved surfaces.


17. TYPES OF SOLAR SYSTEMS
============================

A) On-Grid (Grid-Tied) System
  — Connected to the electricity grid.
  — No battery required.
  — Excess power goes to the grid (net metering).
  — Does NOT work during power cuts (safety feature).
  — Most affordable option.
  — Best for: Urban homes with reliable grid supply.

B) Off-Grid System
  — Completely independent of the grid.
  — Uses battery storage for nighttime/cloudy days.
  — Works during power cuts.
  — More expensive due to battery cost.
  — Best for: Rural areas, farms, remote locations.

C) Hybrid System
  — Connected to the grid AND has battery storage.
  — Best of both worlds — saves money AND provides backup.
  — Works during power cuts.
  — Most expensive upfront.
  — Best for: Areas with frequent power cuts.

21. MAINTENANCE OF SOLAR PANELS
=================================

Solar panels are very low-maintenance. Here's what you need to know:

A) Cleaning (Every 2–4 weeks)
  — Wash panels with water and a soft sponge or cloth.
  — Best time: Early morning when panels are cool and dew-covered.
  — Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive scrubbers.
  — In dusty areas, clean more frequently.
  — Dirty panels can lose 5–25% of their output.

B) Wiring Check (Every 6 months)
  — Inspect cables for wear, corrosion, or loose connections.
  — Check that earthing connections are intact.
  — Look for rodent damage (use conduit protection).

C) Inverter Maintenance
  — Check inverter indicator lights regularly.
  — Keep the inverter in a ventilated, shaded area.
  — Typical lifespan: 10–12 years (micro-inverters: 20+ years).
  — Budget for one replacement during the panel lifetime.

D) Energy Monitoring
  — Use your inverter's app to track daily production.
  — Watch for sudden drops in output (may indicate issues).
  — Many systems send automatic alerts.

E) Professional Inspection (Annual)
  — Schedule one professional check-up per year.
  — Cost: ₹2,000–₹5,000 per year.
  — Covers panel integrity, wiring, inverter, mounting structure.

24. COMMON MYTHS ABOUT SOLAR PANELS
======================================

Myth 1: "Solar panels don't work on cloudy days."
  TRUTH: Solar panels work on LIGHT, not heat. They produce 25–40% output
  on cloudy days. Germany, one of the cloudiest countries, is a top solar
  producer.

Myth 2: "Solar panels are too expensive."
  TRUTH: Prices have dropped 90% in the last decade. After subsidies, a
  3 kW system costs just ₹1.0–₹1.7 lakh — and pays for itself in 3–5 years.

Myth 3: "Maintenance is difficult and expensive."
  TRUTH: No moving parts. Just clean with water every 2–4 weeks. Annual
  maintenance: ₹1,000–₹3,000. Less than a dinner at a restaurant.

Myth 4: "Solar panels damage roofs."
  TRUTH: Properly installed panels actually PROTECT your roof from sun,
  rain, and hail. Modern mounting systems don't penetrate the roof.

Myth 5: "Solar only works in hot climates."
  TRUTH: Solar panels actually work BETTER in cooler temperatures (but with
  sunlight). Extreme heat can slightly reduce efficiency.

Myth 6: "Solar panels are harmful to the environment."
  TRUTH: The energy needed to manufacture a panel is recovered in 1–3 years
  of operation. After that, it produces clean energy for 22+ more years.
  Most panel materials are recyclable.


25. SOLAR INSTALLATION PROCESS — STEP BY STEP
================================================

Step 1: Site Assessment (1 day)
  — A solar engineer visits your property.
  — Checks roof condition, orientation, shading, and available area.
  — Reviews your electricity consumption and sanctioned load.
  — Recommends the optimal system size.

Step 2: System Design (1–3 days)
  — Panel layout is designed for maximum output.
  — Inverter size is calculated.
  — Wiring diagram and mounting plan are prepared.
  — You receive a detailed proposal with costs and expected savings.

Step 3: Installation (1–3 days)
  — Mounting structures are installed on the roof.
  — Solar panels are placed and securely fastened.
  — DC wiring connects panels to the inverter.
  — Inverter is installed (usually on a wall near the electrical panel).
  — AC wiring connects the inverter to your home's electrical system.
  — Earthing and lightning protection are installed.
  — System is tested and commissioned.

Step 4: Grid Connection & Net Meter (1–4 weeks)
  — Your electricity distribution company (DISCOM) inspects the system.
  — A bi-directional net meter is installed.
  — Once approved, you can start exporting excess power to the grid.

Step 5: Monitoring & Handover
  — Remote monitoring is configured (app or web portal).
  — You receive all documentation (warranties, test reports, user manual).
  — Training on basic maintenance is provided.
  — You can now track real-time performance from your phone.

26. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================

Q: Is SolarGenix free to use?
A: Yes! Creating an account and using all features is completely free.

Q: Do I need to install anything?
A: No. SolarGenix is a website that runs in your browser. No downloads or
   installations are required.

Q: Is my data safe?
A: Yes. Your account is protected by a secure authentication system with
   encrypted passwords and JWT tokens.

Q: What is a "pincode" in the Solar Prediction feature?
A: It's your 6-digit Indian postal code (e.g., 380015 for Ahmedabad). The
   system uses this to determine your area's weather and sunlight conditions.

Q: What if my pincode doesn't work?
A: Try using a nearby pincode from the same city or district.

Q: How accurate are the predictions?
A: Our AI models achieve approximately 98.5% accuracy. However, predictions
   are based on historical and average data — actual results may vary based
   on real-time weather conditions.

Q: What is "cycle index" in Bill Prediction?
A: It's the number of months into the future you want to predict. Enter "1"
   for next month, "2" for two months from now, etc.

Q: What does "kW" mean?
A: Kilowatt (kW) is a unit of power. It measures how much electricity a
   solar system can produce at any given moment. A 1 kW system produces
   about 120 units (kWh) of electricity per month in India.

Q: What does "kWh" or "unit" mean?
A: Kilowatt-hour (kWh) is a unit of energy. It's the amount of energy used
   when you run a 1,000-watt appliance for 1 hour. On your electricity bill,
   1 kWh = 1 unit.

Q: How many panels do I need for my home?
A: It depends on your electricity consumption. Use our Bill Optimization
   feature — enter your current bill and target bill, and it will tell you
   exactly how many panels you need.

Q: Can I use SolarGenix on my phone?
A: Yes! The website is responsive and works on phones, tablets, and computers.

Q: What is the chatbot for?
A: The chatbot is an AI assistant that answers your questions about solar
   energy, SolarGenix features, or anything related to solar power. Click the
   orange chat bubble in the bottom-right corner of any page.

Q: I see "Unauthorized" errors when using features. What should I do?
A: Your session may have expired. Log out and log back in.

Q: Can SolarGenix connect to my solar system?
A: Not currently. SolarGenix is a prediction and advisory tool. It doesn't
   connect to physical solar hardware.
